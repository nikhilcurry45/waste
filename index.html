WASTE AND ITS MANAGEMENT


Here we will learn about different types of waste and the methods to control them























DIFFERENT TYPES OF WASTE

Municipal Waste

Its the waste collected and treated by municipalities.These includes food, paper, plastics, textiles, leather, wood, glass, metals, sanitary waste in septic tanks, and other wastes






Industrial Waste

Industrial waste is the waste produced by industrial activity which includes any material that is rendered useless during a manufacturing process for example metals, ashes, chemicals and more.






Hazardous Waste

A waste with properties that make it dangerous or capable of having a harmful effect on human health or the environment. these includes oils, batteries, paints etc.






Electronic Waste

Includes the gadgets which are discarded like mobilephones, computers, lights and other appliances.






IMPACTS OF WASTE

Environment Pollution

Improperly managed waste can lead to pollution of air, water, and soil. For example, the decomposition of organic waste in landfills produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas contributing to climate change.

Health Risks

Poor waste management practices can pose health risks to humans and wildlife. Exposure to hazardous waste or improper disposal of medical waste can lead to contamination and the spread of diseases.

Soil Degradation

Landfills and improper disposal sites can contaminate soil, making it unsuitable for agriculture or other productive uses. The infiltration of harmful substances into the soil can affect crop growth, reduce soil fertility, and contaminate groundwater.

Marine Pollution

Improper disposal of waste, particularly plastics, is a significant contributor to marine pollution. Plastics and other debris can end up in rivers and oceans, harming marine life through ingestion, entanglement, and habitat destruction.

WAYS TO CONTROL WASTE

Managing and controling the waste will not only save the environment but also improve the condition of any area where people are living.

Recovery and Recycling

Converting waste materials into reusable products.

Reduction of waste

Reducing the use of harmful waste materials like plastic.

Construction material

Many waste materials like plastic, wood, glasses can be used for Construction.

Dumping and Landfilling

An area of land can be used for disposal of waste materials..

GOVERMENT POLICIES
Waste To Energy

Encourages the reduction of waste generation and promotes environmentally friendly practices.

Education and awareness

These campaigns aim to inform citizens about proper waste sorting, recycling techniques, and the importance of waste reduction.

Legislation and Regulation

Governments often enact laws and regulations to manage waste effectively.
